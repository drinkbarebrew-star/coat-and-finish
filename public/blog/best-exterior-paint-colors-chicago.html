Best Exterior Paint Colors for Chicago Homes (2026 Trends)

June 18, 2026 • 9 min read • Exterior Painting

Choosing the right exterior paint color for your Chicago home is one of the most impactful decisions you can make as a homeowner. The right color enhances your home's architectural details, boosts curb appeal, and can even increase property value. The wrong color does the opposite, making your home look dated, out of place, or bland.

Chicago's diverse architectural landscape makes color selection both exciting and challenging. A color that looks stunning on a Lincoln Park Victorian might clash with a Wicker Park bungalow. The city's harsh weather also plays a role, as colors need to hold up through scorching summers, bitter winters, and everything in between. Here are the top exterior paint colors Chicago homeowners are choosing in 2026, along with guidance on matching colors to your home's style.

Top 8 Exterior Paint Colors for 2026

1. Classic Charcoal

Dark charcoal remains one of the most popular exterior colors in Chicago, and for good reason. It gives homes a sophisticated, modern edge without the starkness of true black. Charcoal works beautifully on a wide range of architectural styles, from contemporary builds to updated greystones. It pairs well with warm wood accents, brass hardware, and crisp white trim for a striking contrast.

Best for: Modern homes, updated Victorians, contemporary row houses
Try: Benjamin Moore Wrought Iron (2124-10), Sherwin-Williams Iron Ore (SW 7069)

2. Sage Green

Sage green has emerged as the breakout exterior color of 2026. This muted, earthy green connects homes to their natural surroundings and creates a warm, inviting presence on the street. It works particularly well in neighborhoods with mature tree canopies, which describes many of Chicago's most desirable residential areas. Sage green softens the hard lines of brick and stone while adding personality without overwhelming.

Best for: Bungalows, Craftsman homes, cottages
Try: Benjamin Moore Sage Wisdom (2138-40), Sherwin-Williams Clary Sage (SW 6178)

3. Navy Blue

Navy blue continues to be a strong choice for Chicago exteriors. It conveys timeless elegance and pairs naturally with the city's architectural heritage. A deep navy looks particularly striking on homes with white or cream-colored trim, creating a classic combination that never goes out of style. Navy also holds up well against Chicago's grey winter skies, maintaining its richness even on overcast days.

Best for: Georgian homes, Colonial revivals, two-flats
Try: Benjamin Moore Hale Navy (HC-154), Sherwin-Williams Naval (SW 6244)

4. Warm White

Not all whites are created equal, and the trend in 2026 leans heavily toward warm whites rather than cool or stark ones. A warm white with subtle cream or yellow undertones gives homes a fresh, clean appearance without feeling sterile. This is an especially popular choice for brick homes that have been limewashed or painted, creating a bright, airy look that photographs beautifully and reflects natural light.

Best for: Brick homes, farmhouse styles, any architecture that benefits from a clean canvas
Try: Benjamin Moore White Dove (OC-17), Sherwin-Williams Alabaster (SW 7008)

5. Greige

Greige, the sophisticated blend of grey and beige, continues to dominate Chicago exteriors in 2026. It offers the neutrality of grey with the warmth of beige, creating a balanced, approachable look that complements nearly every neighborhood. Greige is especially effective on homes where you want to update the exterior without making a bold color statement. It serves as an excellent backdrop for colorful doors and landscaping.

Best for: Any architectural style, especially transitional and updated traditional homes
Try: Benjamin Moore Revere Pewter (HC-172), Sherwin-Williams Agreeable Gray (SW 7029)

6. Forest Green

Deeper than sage but not as dark as hunter green, forest green is gaining momentum as a bold exterior choice. This color makes a strong statement while still feeling grounded and natural. It pairs beautifully with cream or gold trim and looks especially handsome on homes with stone or brick accents. In neighborhoods with lots of greenery, a forest green exterior creates a harmonious connection between the built and natural environments.

Best for: Victorians, Arts and Crafts homes, homes with stone accents
Try: Benjamin Moore Essex Green (HC-188), Sherwin-Williams Rookwood Dark Green (SW 2816)

7. Slate Blue

Slate blue sits in the sweet spot between blue and grey, offering a versatile color that feels both calming and distinctive. It is less common than navy, which means your home will stand out on the block without feeling out of place. Slate blue works well with both warm and cool accent colors, giving you flexibility with trim, doors, and shutters.

Best for: Cape Cod styles, Colonial homes, bungalows
Try: Benjamin Moore Van Deusen Blue (HC-156), Sherwin-Williams Granite Peak (SW 6250)

8. Cream and Butter Yellow

Warm cream and soft butter yellow tones are making a comeback in 2026, particularly on smaller homes and bungalows. These colors radiate warmth and cheerfulness, making them ideal for homes on tree-lined residential streets. They also work well with darker trim colors like forest green or charcoal, creating a classic combination that has been popular in Chicago neighborhoods for over a century.

Best for: Bungalows, cottages, smaller homes
Try: Benjamin Moore Hawthorne Yellow (HC-4), Sherwin-Williams Butter Up (SW 6681)

Choosing Colors by Architectural Style

Chicago is home to an extraordinary range of architectural styles, and choosing colors that complement your home's character is essential. Here is a quick guide:

Victorian and Queen Anne

Chicago's Victorians are known for their ornate details, and the right color scheme can highlight those features beautifully. Multi-color palettes with three or even four coordinated colors are traditional and appropriate. Consider a dark body color like charcoal or forest green with contrasting trim and accent colors that draw attention to brackets, spindles, and window surrounds.

Chicago Bungalow

The Chicago bungalow is an architectural icon, and its low-slung horizontal profile benefits from colors that emphasize its width. Warm, earthy tones like sage green, greige, or butter yellow complement the brick and stone that typically form the lower portion of the facade. Keep trim colors light to create a sense of openness.

Georgian and Colonial Revival

These formal, symmetrical homes look best in classic color combinations. Navy or charcoal with white trim is the most timeless choice, but a deep red or forest green can also work well. The key is to maintain a sense of formality and balance that matches the home's proportions.

Modern and Contemporary

Modern homes in Chicago lean toward monochromatic palettes with minimal contrast. Charcoal, dark grey, warm white, and black are the most common choices. If you want to add color, consider a bold accent on the front door or garage door rather than the main body of the home.

Tips for Choosing the Right Color

Selecting an exterior color from a small swatch at the paint store is one of the most common mistakes homeowners make. Here are some practical tips to help you make the right choice:

  • Test in different light. Paint large sample areas (at least 2 by 2 feet) on different sides of your home. Colors look dramatically different in morning light versus afternoon sun, and the north side of your home receives different light than the south side.
  • Consider your fixed elements. Your roof color, brick or stone accents, and hardscape materials are not changing. Your new paint color needs to work with all of them. Bring photos of these elements to the paint store.
  • Look at your neighbors. You do not need to match the houses around you, but your color should feel like it belongs in the neighborhood. Drive through the area and note which homes look the best to get a sense of what works.
  • Think about maintenance. Darker colors show dust and pollen more readily but hide stains. Lighter colors stay cooler in summer but show dirt more easily. Consider how much maintenance you are willing to do.
  • Do not forget the door. Your front door is an opportunity to add personality. A bold door color like red, yellow, or teal can make even a neutral exterior feel distinctive and welcoming.
  • Get a professional opinion. A color consultation with a professional painter can save you from an expensive mistake. An experienced painter has seen thousands of homes and can guide you toward combinations that work in your specific neighborhood and with your specific architecture.
